# Originality And Safety

Always use this reference before finalizing output.

## Non-Imitation Rule

This skill provides reusable writing mechanisms. It must not reproduce or closely imitate the source author.

Do not:

- claim to write as 咪蒙 or in the author's exact style
- reuse source titles
- reuse source sentences, paragraph sequences, metaphors, jokes, or distinctive turns
- create a close paraphrase of any known article
- preserve a source article's structure while merely changing nouns
- include source text in the skill output unless the user provided a short excerpt and asks for analysis

When a user asks for imitation, redirect:

`我不能复刻某位作者本人的表达，但可以用高情绪公众号长文的通用机制，写一篇原创版本。`

## Originality Procedure

Before finalizing:

1. Restate the user's topic in your own brief.
2. Choose a new angle, not an article-shaped memory.
3. Generate fresh titles from molds, then reject any title that feels like a remembered source title.
4. Draft from the user's topic, facts, and target reader.
5. Replace any sentence that feels too aphoristic or familiar with a more specific scene or action.
6. Avoid naming the source author in the final article unless the user is asking about the skill itself.

## Factual Safety

- Verify current facts, legal claims, medical claims, financial claims, and named public events when accuracy matters.
- If verification is unavailable, frame statements as opinion, hypothetical, or user-provided context.
- Do not invent reader submissions, quotes, statistics, or news details.
- For composite examples, label them as composites or write them as fictional scenes.

## Target Safety

Strong language may target behaviors, norms, excuses, systems, or arguments. It should not target protected traits, private individuals, vulnerable people, or unverifiable allegations.

Avoid:

- dehumanizing labels
- body shaming
- misogyny, racism, homophobia, transphobia, ableism, or class contempt
- harassment toward a private person
- diagnosing someone else's mental health as an insult

Prefer:

- `这种行为的问题是...`
- `这套规则真正保护的是...`
- `你可以拒绝这笔情绪债。`
- `这不是爱，是控制。`
- `这不是成熟，是把委屈包装成懂事。`

## Final Checklist

The output is ready only if all answers are yes:

- Is it original, not a close imitation or rewrite?
- Does the title promise a specific conflict?
- Does the opening contain a concrete hook within 150 Chinese characters?
- Does the article move from scene to judgment to permission?
- Are strong claims supported by scenes, reasoning, or user-provided facts?
- Are current or factual claims verified or safely framed?
- Does the ending give the reader a boundary, permission, or useful verdict?
